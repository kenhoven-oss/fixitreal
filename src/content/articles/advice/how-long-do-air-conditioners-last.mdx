**Average AC lifespans by type, 2026:**

| Type | Expected lifespan |
|---|---|
| Central AC condenser (outdoor unit) | 12–18 years |
| Central AC indoor coil | 15–20 years |
| Heat pump (used for both heating + cooling) | 12–18 years |
| Mini-split (ductless) | 15–20 years |
| Window unit | 8–12 years |
| Portable AC | 5–10 years |

**Add 2–4 years** for annual maintenance and clean coils.  
**Subtract 4–7 years** for coastal saltwater locations.  
**Subtract 2–3 years** for hot climates with year-round heavy duty.

**Replacement threshold:** any repair quote over half the replacement cost OR multiple failures in 18 months on a unit 12+ years old.
</QuickAnswer>

## What limits AC lifespan

### Compressor health

The outdoor compressor is the most expensive single component ($800–$1,800 part + labor). Heat, vibration, and dirty coils kill compressors. Most AC units die from compressor failure on units 12+ years old. Once the compressor fails, repair cost approaches full replacement on most systems.

### Refrigerant leaks

Slow refrigerant leaks at fittings, lineset connections, or coils are common as units age. Each leak repair is $400–$1,200; multiple leak repairs in 2 years usually means replacement makes more sense than chasing leaks.

### Corrosion

Outdoor units rust from the outside in. Coastal saltwater accelerates this — units 5 miles from the coast can show significant corrosion at 8 years; identical units inland may look fine at 15.

### Coil scaling

Indoor evaporator coils accumulate biofilm and debris over years. Cleaning helps; eventually the coil corrodes through. Coil replacement is $800–$1,500 in part + labor — often a tipping point for full replacement on older systems.

### Hard usage

Year-round cooling demand (Phoenix, Miami, Houston) puts AC units on a duty cycle 3–4× higher than seasonal-cooling climates. Lifespan correspondingly shorter.

## The 5 warning signs of end-of-life

1. **Yearly refrigerant top-offs.** A small annual leak is a chronic leak that gets worse. Each top-off is $250–$550; the leak isn't going to heal.

2. **Capacitor failures multiple times in 2 years.** The capacitor doesn't fail randomly — it fails because compressor stress is degrading it. Repeated capacitor swaps point to compressor end-of-life.

3. **Electric bills rising 10%+ with no usage change.** Aging compressors lose efficiency. Same cooling output costs more.

4. **Compressor noise getting louder, especially on startup.** Bearings wearing, valves not seating cleanly. Sound predicts failure within 1–3 cooling seasons.

5. **The unit is past warranty age** (most are 5–10 years parts warranty). Parts may still be available but you're funding all of it.

## Repair vs replace decision

### Under 10 years old

Repair anything reasonable. Capacitor, contactor, fan motor, refrigerant top-off (if leak found and fixed), evaporator coil, even compressor for relatively new units still under labor warranty.

### 10–12 years old

Repair single failures under $1,000. Refrigerant leak repair under $800. Coil replacement makes sense if under $1,500 and the rest of the system is healthy.

### 12+ years old

Get a replacement quote before approving any repair over $1,000. The "half rule" — if repair exceeds half the replacement cost, replace.

R-22 refrigerant units (most pre-2010) almost always favor replacement on any major issue — R-22 reclaim costs are $100+/lb, and the unit is by definition past its design refrigerant transition.

### Multiple failures within 18 months

Even on a younger unit, three service calls in a season usually means the system has reached its useful end. Statistically, the next failure is close.

## When proactive replacement makes sense

- AC is 12+ years old and you're remodeling — replace during the disruption.
- Federal tax credits change the math (especially for heat pumps — up to $2,000 through 2032).
- Your electric utility offers rebates expiring on a deadline.
- You're switching from gas furnace + AC to a heat pump (eliminates one system, qualifies for bigger credits).
- The unit is loud enough to be a nuisance — modern variable-speed units are dramatically quieter.

## Extend your AC's lifespan

The 4 highest-impact maintenance tasks:

1. **Change the air filter on schedule.** See [our filter schedule guide](/advice/how-often-to-change-air-filter).
2. **Clear the outdoor condenser annually.** Rinse fins gently with a garden hose. Pull weeds/debris within 2 feet.
3. **Have refrigerant pressure checked annually.** Catches small leaks before they damage the compressor.
4. **Clean the evaporator coil every 2–3 years.** Reduces airflow restriction and biofilm.

Total maintenance cost: $150–$300/year. Compared to a $7,000 replacement, it's the cheapest insurance.
