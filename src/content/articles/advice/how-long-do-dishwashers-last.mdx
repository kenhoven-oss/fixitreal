<QuickAnswer>
**Dishwasher lifespans by tier, 2026:**

| Tier | Expected lifespan |
|---|---|
| Budget (sub-$500 brands) | 7–9 years |
| Mid-range ($600–$900) | 9–13 years |
| Premium (Bosch, Miele, etc.) | 13–17 years |

**Hard water shortens life by 2–3 years.** Rinse aid + soft water adds 2–3 years.

**Replace when:** any major repair exceeds half the replacement cost OR the unit is over 12 years old with multiple recent issues.
</QuickAnswer>

## What limits dishwasher lifespan

### Drain pump and recirculation motor

Highest-failure component. Motors run hot during cycles; over years the bearings wear and the impeller cracks. A failed pump means the dishwasher either fills with water (drain failure) or doesn't spray (recirc failure).

Repair cost: $150–$300 part + $100–$200 labor = $250–$500 all-in. Worth it on machines under 10 years old.

### Control board

The brain of the dishwasher. Failures usually appear as buttons not responding, cycle errors, or random shutdowns. Control boards cost $200–$400 part + $100–$200 labor. On units 10+ years old, a control board failure usually tips toward replacement.

### Heating element

Hard water mineral buildup insulates the heating element. Over years the element fails. Cost: $50–$100 part + $100–$150 labor = $150–$250 all-in. Usually a single repair extends life 3–5 years.

### Tub seals and door gaskets

The rubber seals around the door and along the tub edges harden and crack over years. Symptoms: water on the floor, especially during fill cycle. Seal replacements are $30–$80 in part, 30-min DIY for most homeowners.

### Plastic tub failures (budget units)

Some budget dishwashers use plastic interior tubs that crack at the seams as plastic ages and cycles. A cracked tub effectively totals the unit — replacement is usually the answer. Stainless steel tubs don't crack and contribute significantly to premium-brand longevity.

## The 5 warning signs of end-of-life

1. **Multiple errors / no-start cycles.** Control electronics degrading.

2. **Standing water 2+ cups at the bottom after each cycle.** Drain pump failing or drain hose kinked.

3. **Visible cracks in the tub or door seal.** Especially at corners and where the door meets the tub.

4. **Dishes coming out gritty or soap-scummed.** Spray arm clog or recirculation motor weakening.

5. **Unusual noise (grinding, screeching).** Bearings going on motors or pump.

Any **two** together on a 10+ year machine = budget for replacement. **Three or more** = replace this year.

## Repair vs replace decision

### Under 8 years old

Repair almost anything. The replacement cost won't recoup before the new unit hits the same age.

### 8–11 years old

The "half rule" — if the repair exceeds half the replacement cost, replace. Mid-tier replacements run $600–$900 installed, so $300–$450 is the repair-vs-replace tipping point.

### 12+ years old

Get a replacement quote before approving any repair over $200. The unit is past expected service life; the next failure follows close.

### Premium brand (Bosch, Miele)

These last 13–17 years routinely. Repair anything reasonable up to year 12. Even major repairs often pencil out — and parts availability is usually better than budget brands at the same age.

## When to upgrade tier instead of replacing same-tier

If you're replacing a budget unit that lasted 7 years, the next budget unit will likely last 7 years too. A mid-tier replacement adds 3–5 years for $200–$400 more. The math usually favors going up.

If you're replacing a mid-tier unit that lasted 11 years, the math gets closer. A premium unit costs $300–$700 more but adds 4–7 years.

The dollar-per-year-of-service comparison:

- **$500 budget unit lasting 7 years = $71/year**
- **$800 mid-tier unit lasting 11 years = $73/year**
- **$1,400 premium unit lasting 15 years = $93/year**

Premium isn't cheaper per year, but it's quieter, dries better, and is more reliable cycle-to-cycle. Lifestyle vs cost tradeoff.

## Extend your dishwasher's life

### Four highest-impact maintenance habits

1. **Pre-run hot water at the sink.** Run the kitchen faucet on hot for 30 seconds before starting the dishwasher. Hot water arrives at the unit immediately, reducing cycle time and heating-element strain.

2. **Use rinse aid.** $5/year. Reduces mineral buildup on heating element, spray arms, and seals.

3. **Clean the filter monthly.** Most dishwashers have a removable filter at the bottom. Pull, rinse, replace. Takes 90 seconds. Skipping this is the #1 cause of premature drain pump failure.

4. **Clean spray arms.** Pull off (usually a twist), check for clogged spray holes. Use a toothpick or wire to clear mineral buildup. Once a year for soft water; quarterly for hard water.

### What not to do

- **Don't pre-rinse dishes** unless they have caked food. Modern detergents need food particles to bind to (they're enzymatic). Pre-rinsing wastes water without extending dishwasher life.

- **Don't overload.** Cycles can't clean dishes that block spray arms. The bottom rack should not have items extending above its rim. Result of overloading: re-runs, double water use, double wear.

- **Don't use too much detergent.** More isn't better — modern detergents are concentrated. Excess detergent causes residue, faster seal wear, and clogged drains.

## When to call a pro

- Standing water and a clear drain (drain pump failing).
- Won't start, won't drain, won't fill — multiple cycles broken.
- Visible water leak from the door or tub.
- Burning smell during operation (electrical issue — stop using immediately).

Appliance repair typical service call: $100–$200 trip + diagnostic. Most repairs $200–$500 all-in.
