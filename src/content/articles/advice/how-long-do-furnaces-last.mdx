<QuickAnswer>
**Average furnace lifespans by type, 2026:**

| Type | Expected lifespan |
|---|---|
| Modern gas (95% AFUE) | 15–20 years |
| Older gas (80% AFUE) | 20–30 years (but inefficient) |
| Electric furnace | 20–30 years |
| Oil furnace | 15–25 years |
| Heat pump (year-round heat + cool) | 12–18 years |

**Add 2–4 years** for annual maintenance.  
**Subtract 3–5 years** for skipped service and clogged filters.  
**Heat exchanger failures** can occur at any age but are most common after year 12.

**Critical safety:** any furnace with a confirmed cracked heat exchanger should not be operated until repaired or replaced. CO risk.
</QuickAnswer>

<SafetyWarning>
Gas furnaces produce carbon monoxide during combustion. A cracked heat exchanger, blocked vent, or improper installation can leak CO into the home — odorless, can be fatal. Every home with a gas furnace needs working CO detectors on every floor. Replace detector batteries twice yearly; replace the units themselves every 7–10 years (most have an expiration date printed on them).
</SafetyWarning>

## What limits furnace lifespan

### Heat exchanger condition

The heat exchanger is a metal chamber where combustion happens. Combustion gases pass through the inside; house air passes around the outside. Repeated heating/cooling cycles eventually crack the metal — a serious safety issue (CO can leak through cracks into the air your furnace circulates).

Heat exchangers typically last 18–25 years before crack risk becomes significant. Newer high-efficiency units use stainless steel heat exchangers (better) vs older aluminized steel.

### Burner condition

Burners get sooted up over years, especially in homes with restrictive air filters or dirty combustion air. Annual cleaning extends burner life significantly. Skipped maintenance = degraded combustion = more wear on every component downstream.

### Inducer motor and blower

These move air through the furnace. Both have bearings and motor windings that wear over time. Typical service life: 10–15 years. Replacements are mid-range repairs ($400–$900 each).

### Electronic controls

Circuit boards and sensors that govern furnace operation. Service life is typically 12–20 years. Replacement boards run $200–$500 in part plus labor.

## The 6 warning signs

By the time these appear, you're in the replacement-budget window.

### 1. Rising gas bills with no usage change

Aging furnaces lose efficiency. An 80% AFUE furnace that loses 5 percentage points over the years now delivers 75% of the gas's heat to the house — 25% goes up the flue. The bill climbs noticeably over multiple winters.

### 2. Yellow flame instead of blue

A healthy gas flame burns mostly blue with occasional yellow tips. Predominantly yellow flame indicates incomplete combustion — produces more CO, soots up the heat exchanger, signals burner or air supply issues.

This is a "call HVAC today" symptom even if the furnace is still running. Yellow flame = CO risk.

### 3. Short cycling

Healthy furnaces run cycles of 5–15 minutes each. Short cycling (cycles under 3 minutes, on/off multiple times an hour) means something is wrong:
- Failing flame sensor.
- Failing high-limit switch (overheating).
- Clogged filter restricting airflow.
- Oversized furnace (rare; install issue).

Short cycling kills furnaces — components wear at thermal-cycle rates much faster than design.

### 4. Banging, popping, or rumbling sounds

- **Banging at startup:** delayed ignition. Gas builds up briefly before lighting. CO risk and potentially explosive. Call HVAC.
- **Popping during heating:** sediment in the heat exchanger or burner contamination.
- **Rumbling:** burner running poorly (dirty or wrong gas pressure).

None of these are normal. All warrant a service call.

### 5. Increased dust around vents

Aging furnaces have aging blowers and air handlers. Seals fail, internal surfaces get dirty, biofilm grows in the cabinet. The end result: more dust being blown into the house.

### 6. The unit is past warranty age

Most residential furnaces carry 10-year parts warranties and 20-year heat-exchanger warranties. A furnace at year 18+ is past most major warranty coverage — repair cost is now all yours.

## Repair vs. replace decision

### Under 12 years old

Repair most things. Hot-surface ignitor ($150–$300), flame sensor ($100–$200), inducer motor ($400–$900), control board ($300–$600) all make sense.

### 12–18 years old

Repair single failures under $800. Skip the heat-pump-vs-furnace upgrade decision until a major failure forces it.

### 18+ years old

Get a [replacement quote](/costs/furnace-replacement-cost) before any repair over $500. The unit is past expected service life; the next failure will likely follow within 1–3 winters.

### Heat exchanger cracked

Replace the furnace. Some units have warranty coverage on the heat exchanger itself, but labor isn't covered ($800–$1,500). For a furnace over 12 years old, replacement is almost always smarter than chasing a heat-exchanger repair.

## When to upgrade to heat pump instead

For furnace 15+ years old AND AC also approaching end-of-life: a [heat pump](/costs/heat-pump-cost) replaces both systems with one unit. Federal tax credits up to $2,000 (heat pump) + state and utility rebates often make the math work in favor of the heat pump over a same-type furnace replacement.

For very cold climates (sustained sub-zero), consider dual-fuel: heat pump + small gas furnace backup. See [heat pump vs furnace](/advice/heat-pump-vs-furnace) for the full comparison.

## Best time to replace

**Off-season is cheaper.** Replace April through August:
- Demand is low — HVAC companies offer 15–25% off vs peak.
- Lead times are short (1–2 weeks vs 4–8 weeks peak).
- Time to commission the system before winter demand.

**Worst time:** January–February. Peak demand, highest pricing, emergency-rate possible if your unit fails in February.

If your furnace is 15+ years old in April, get quotes now — even if you replace 2 years later, you'll know the market.

## Extend lifespan

Three highest-impact maintenance items:

1. **Change the air filter on schedule.** See our [filter schedule guide](/advice/how-often-to-change-air-filter).
2. **Annual cleaning + combustion analysis** ($120–$220 typical, often part of an HVAC maintenance plan).
3. **Keep the furnace cabinet free of dust and obstructions.** Don't store anything within 24 inches of the unit.

Total cost: under $250/year. Pays for itself in efficiency and avoided emergency calls.
