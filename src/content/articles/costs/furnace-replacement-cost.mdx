Replacing a furnace is one of the higher-stakes home repair decisions — wrong sizing, wrong venting, or a skipped permit creates real safety problems years down the line. Here's what a fair quote looks like in 2026 and where the bills get padded.

<QuickAnswer>
A standard 80,000–100,000 BTU **95% AFUE gas furnace replacement** runs **$3,800–$7,500 installed** in 2026, including unit, labor, permit, and code-required items. **Electric furnace** is cheaper to install ($2,800–$5,500) but runs 2–3× the operating cost over the unit's life. **Conversion from 80% to 95% AFUE** adds $400–$900 for new sidewall PVC venting and a condensate line. Any quote above **$9,000 on a basic like-for-like 95% AFUE swap** without major venting or gas-line work warrants a second quote. **Permits are non-negotiable** — skipping them can void insurance if the furnace later fails.
</QuickAnswer>

<SafetyWarning>
Gas furnaces produce carbon monoxide as combustion exhaust. A cracked heat exchanger, blocked vent, or improper installation can leak CO into the home — which is odorless and can be fatal. Every home with a gas furnace needs a working CO detector on every floor. Test detectors twice a year. After any furnace install, confirm the technician tested the heat exchanger and verified vent draft before leaving.
</SafetyWarning>

## What you're actually paying for

### Unit (the equipment)

- **80% AFUE gas furnace:** $700–$1,500 wholesale. Cheapest, vents through existing metal flue. Federal minimum in southern states only.
- **95% AFUE gas furnace (condensing):** $1,200–$2,200 wholesale. Federal minimum in northern states. Requires PVC sidewall venting + condensate drain.
- **Two-stage gas furnace:** $1,800–$3,200 wholesale. Quieter, more even heating, modest efficiency gain.
- **Modulating / variable-speed:** $2,800–$4,500 wholesale. Premium tier. Worth it only in larger or zoned homes.
- **Electric furnace:** $700–$1,800 wholesale. Cheap to install, expensive to operate.

Contractors mark up 1.3×–1.7× wholesale. A $1,500 unit lands on your quote at $2,000–$2,500.

### Labor

- **Standard install (existing ductwork, same fuel type, basic location):** 4–8 hours, $1,200–$2,400 labor.
- **Conversion install (80% → 95% AFUE, new venting):** 6–12 hours, $1,800–$3,500 labor.
- **Relocation install (new location, new gas line, new vent path):** 1–2 days, $3,000–$6,500 labor.

### Code-required items

- **Permit:** $75–$300 in most jurisdictions.
- **Sidewall PVC venting** (for 95% AFUE): $200–$500 materials + install.
- **Condensate drain** (for 95% AFUE): $50–$150.
- **Condensate pump** (if no gravity drain available): $200–$400.
- **Gas-line work** (if the existing line is undersized or in poor condition): $200–$800.
- **Electrical** (new disconnect, sometimes new circuit): $150–$400.

### Optional add-ons

- **Smart thermostat:** $150–$350.
- **Whole-house humidifier:** $400–$800 install.
- **Media filter upgrade:** $250–$500.
- **UV light:** $300–$600.

## Cost by BTU rating

Furnaces are sized in BTU output (British Thermal Units). Rough sizing for typical insulated homes:

| Home size (sq ft) | BTU output | 95% AFUE install (typical) |
|---|---|---|
| 600–1,000 | 40,000 BTU | $3,500–$5,000 |
| 1,000–1,500 | 60,000 BTU | $4,000–$5,800 |
| 1,500–2,000 | 80,000 BTU | $4,800–$6,800 |
| 2,000–2,500 | 100,000 BTU | $5,500–$7,500 |
| 2,500–3,500 | 120,000 BTU | $6,500–$8,500 |

Climate adds 25%+ to BTU needs in cold-state metros (MN, WI, ND, MT) and reduces them in warm-state metros (FL, TX, AZ). A Manual J load calculation is the proper sizing tool; if the contractor isn't doing one, they're guessing.

## AFUE efficiency — what's worth paying for

<PullQuote>The $700 you save buying an 80% AFUE furnace becomes $200–$400 more in annual gas bills. Over a 15-year service life, you've paid the savings back three times.</PullQuote>

- **80% AFUE**: federal minimum in southern states. 20% of fuel goes up the flue. Buy only if you're staying in the house under 3 years AND in a warm climate.
- **90% AFUE**: rare bracket; the market jumped from 80% to 95%. Avoid.
- **95% AFUE**: federal minimum in northern states. Sweet spot for most homes. Requires PVC venting + condensate drain.
- **96–98% AFUE**: marginal further gain. The premium often doesn't pay back.

Federal tax credits via the Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit refund up to $600 on qualifying high-efficiency gas furnaces through 2032. Some utility rebates stack on top.

## Why 95% AFUE adds venting cost

An 80% AFUE furnace pushes hot exhaust out a metal flue through the roof — same as boilers and water heaters used to. A 95% AFUE furnace extracts so much heat from combustion that the exhaust is cool enough to condense water vapor. That changes two things:

1. **Venting:** PVC pipe (cheaper, no clearances to combustibles) replaces metal flue. New sidewall vent + intake.
2. **Condensate:** The unit produces 1–3 gallons of acidic water per day during heating season. Needs a drain (gravity or pump).

A first-time 80% → 95% conversion adds $400–$900 to the install. Subsequent 95% replacements reuse the venting, so the cost stays in the baseline range.

## What inflates the bill

- **Gas-line "upgrades"** that aren't needed. The existing line is usually fine for a same-BTU replacement. Confirm the contractor's recommendation against the gas-line sizing chart in the install manual.
- **Crane fees** for non-rooftop units. Legitimate if rooftop; padding if not.
- **"Combustion analysis"** as a separate charge. Should be included in install; some shops bill $200+ extra.
- **Permit handling fees** beyond what the city charges. The permit itself is real cost ($75–$300); a $200 handling fee on top is padding.
- **Refrigerant** charges on a furnace install. There's no refrigerant in a furnace. If you see a refrigerant line item, ask what it's for.

## What a fair quote looks like

A typical 80,000 BTU 95% AFUE replacement, like-for-like swap with existing venting:

- Unit: $2,400
- Install labor: $1,800
- Permit: $150
- Vent + condensate materials (refreshed): $250
- Disposal: $100
- **Total: $4,700** (range: $4,200–$5,800 acceptable)

A first-time 80% → 95% conversion at the same BTU:

- Unit: $2,400
- Install labor: $2,500
- Permit: $200
- New PVC vent system: $500
- Condensate drain + pump: $400
- Disposal: $100
- **Total: $6,100** (range: $5,500–$7,200 acceptable)

## When to repair instead

For a furnace under 12 years old:
- Ignitor / flame sensor / capacitor ($150–$400): repair.
- Inducer motor or draft inducer ($400–$900): repair.
- Heat exchanger crack ($1,200–$2,500 for the part alone, plus labor): often a write-off — heat exchangers are warranty items but labor isn't covered.

For a furnace 15+ years old:
- Any major repair over $1,000: replace. The next failure is statistically close, and 80% AFUE units are paying you back to upgrade.

If a contractor says the heat exchanger is cracked, get a second opinion. Cracked-heat-exchanger diagnoses are sometimes legitimate, sometimes a sales tactic. A licensed inspector or a different contractor can confirm with a combustion analysis and visual inspection.
