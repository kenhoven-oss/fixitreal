Drywall repair pricing scales weirdly with hole size because most of the cost is labor setup, not materials. A $20 hole and a $200 hole both need the contractor to show up, set up, prep, and clean up.

<QuickAnswer>
A small drywall repair (under 3 inches) runs **$30–$100 DIY** or **$150–$300 hired** (essentially a service-call minimum). A medium repair (doorknob hole to 12 inches) runs **$40 DIY** or **$250–$500 hired**. A large repair (12 inches to 4×4 ft, with backing and texture matching) runs **$100 DIY** or **$400–$700 hired**. Beyond that, you&rsquo;re into replacement territory at **$1.50–$3.50/sq ft + finish**.

## DIY vs. hire

Drywall repair is one of the most DIY-friendly trades — the materials are cheap, the work is forgiving (mistakes can be sanded out), and the consequences of a bad job are cosmetic, not structural. But DIY isn&rsquo;t free in time:

- **Small repairs (under 3 inches)**: genuinely DIY. 30 minutes of active work + 1 day of drying. Spackle, sand, paint, done. Hiring a handyman to do this is paying $200 to save 30 minutes.
- **Medium repairs (3–12 inches)**: still DIY for confident homeowners. Set aside a weekend — work over 3 evenings to allow each coat of compound to dry properly. The skill is feathering the edges so the patch isn&rsquo;t a raised spot on the wall.
- **Large repairs (12 inches+)**: borderline DIY. The texture-matching is where most DIY attempts go wrong. If the wall is smooth (no texture), still DIY-able. If there&rsquo;s a knockdown or orange-peel texture, hiring a finisher is often worth the cost — they have the tools and the muscle memory.
- **Anything involving water damage or hidden mold**: hire it out. The drywall repair itself is the smallest part of the cost; the source of the water and any remediation work matter more.

## What a fair quote should include

A defensible quote for drywall repair work covers:

1. **Hole size or repair area** stated specifically (in inches or sq ft)
2. **Hourly rate or service-call minimum** plus expected hours
3. **Texture matching** noted (or "smooth wall, no texture")
4. **Paint scope**: patch-paint only, or full-wall repaint, or no paint (you handle)
5. **Cleanup**: drywall sanding is dusty work; a fair quote includes cleanup and dust containment
6. **Number of visits required** — proper joint compound work needs 2–3 visits for full cure cycles

## Red flags

- **One-visit quote on a medium-or-larger repair.** Real joint compound needs 6–12 hours between coats. A one-visit quote means rushed work, which means a visible patch.
- **Quote over $500 for a hole under 6 inches** without water-damage or texture-specialty work in the scope. Overpriced.
- **No mention of the texture** on the existing wall. The contractor is going to surprise you with a "texture match upcharge."
