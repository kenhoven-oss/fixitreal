A typical drywall patch in 2026 costs $50–$400 depending on hole size, location, and finish quality. Small patches (under 4 inches) cost almost nothing in DIY parts and $80–$200 hired. Large patches (12+ inches) or ceiling repairs push toward $300–$600 hired. The biggest cost driver isn't the hole — it's the trip-fee minimum, drop-cloth setup, and multi-coat finish work that takes a patch from "visible mess" to "invisible."

## Cost by hole size

| Hole size | DIY parts cost | Hired cost |
|---|---|---|
| Nail/screw hole (under 1 inch) | $0–$5 | $80–$150 (minimum charge) |
| Small patch (1–4 inches) | $8–$15 | $80–$200 |
| Medium patch (4–12 inches) | $20–$40 | $150–$350 |
| Large patch (12+ inches) | $40–$80 | $250–$600 |
| Full panel replacement (4×8 ft) | $30–$60 | $350–$800 |
| Ceiling patch (medium) | $30–$60 | $250–$500 |
| Water-damaged drywall replacement | varies | $400–$1,500+ (per area) |

DIY parts cost assumes the homeowner already owns basic tools (taping knives, sanding block). Add $30–$80 the first time if you need to buy them.

## What you're paying for hired

The cost breakdown on a typical hired drywall patch is mostly NOT the patch itself:

- **Trip / minimum charge:** $80–$200 (1–2 hour minimum that applies even to small jobs)
- **Drop cloths, dust containment:** 15–30 min
- **The patch itself:** 15–45 min
- **First coat of mud, drying time:** technician comes back the next day
- **Second + third coats:** 2–3 more visits, sometimes consolidated into one
- **Sanding + paint touch-up:** the last visit

A "quick patch" that takes 15 minutes of skilled labor often spans 3–5 days of total project time. The minimum charge covers all of that overhead.

## When DIY makes sense

- **Small patches** (under 4 inches). A pre-mixed patch kit + 5-minute YouTube video gets you to acceptable.
- **Multiple small patches** at once. Bundle them and do all in one Saturday.
- **Patches that'll be painted over** anyway. Texture-matching is the hard part — flat-finish patches are easier than knock-down or skip-trowel textures.

For the full DIY-vs-hire decision, see [Should I repair drywall myself?](/diy-or-hire/repair-drywall).

## When to hire

- **Textured walls** (knock-down, skip-trowel, popcorn ceilings). Matching texture is a specialty skill; DIY attempts often look worse than the original damage.
- **Ceiling patches.** Working overhead, sanding overhead, and managing drop is much harder than wall work. The cost premium is fair.
- **Water-damaged areas** where mold or rot needs assessment. The drywall is the symptom; the cause needs diagnosis first.
- **Full panel replacement.** Cutting, fitting, taping, and finishing a full 4×8 sheet for a clean result is real skill.

## Common pricing red flags

- **"Wall patch + paint + texture match" bundled at $800+ for one small repair.** That's a half-day of work plus minimum-charge padding, not $800 of value. Get a second quote.
- **"Mold testing" added to a water-damage drywall patch without visible mold.** Testing is for visible problems or chronic moisture, not every wet wall.
- **No itemized breakdown.** Patch / coat / sand / paint should each appear; a single "drywall work — $X" line is padding-friendly.
- **Pressure to do "additional cosmetic repairs we noticed."** Discuss any scope additions with you in writing before doing them, not as a surprise on the invoice.

## How to cut the cost

1. **Bundle.** Five small patches in one visit beats five visits.
2. **Provide the paint.** Match the color exactly (bring a chip to the paint store). Avoids the contractor's color-match guesswork.
3. **DIY the small ones.** Pre-mixed patch kits handle 80% of homeowner drywall damage at $10-$15 per kit.
4. **Get three quotes** for anything over $400. Within a metro, drywall pricing varies 40–60% for the same job.

## When patching beats replacement

Patching wins when:
- Damage is localized (one or two holes)
- The wall is in good overall condition
- The patch will be painted over

Replacement wins when:
- Water damage extends past the visible area (use a [moisture meter](/tools/best-moisture-meters-for-homeowners) to confirm)
- The wall has multiple old failed patches creating a quilted look
- Major settling or framing damage suggests the underlying structure changed
- A full kitchen or bathroom remodel is happening anyway

## What I'd do

For a single small patch: buy a $12 patch kit at the hardware store, watch one good YouTube video, do it yourself in 30 minutes plus drying time. Almost every homeowner can.

For a single medium patch on a textured wall, or a ceiling patch: hire it. The texture match alone is worth the minimum charge.

For a list of 4–6 small holes from picture-hanging, baseboard scrapes, or door knobs through walls: either bundle them with one handyman visit ($200–$400 total) or DIY all in one Saturday.
