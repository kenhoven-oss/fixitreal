<QuickAnswer>
Licensed Canadian plumbers in 2026 typically charge **CA$80–$160/hour** for residential service, clustered around **$110–$130/hr** in major metros. Add a **$75–$150 service call fee**, a one-hour minimum, plus **HST/GST/PST** depending on province. A typical fixture repair runs **CA$200–$400 all-in**. Emergency, weekend, and overnight rates run **1.5×–3×** weekday rates. Toronto and Vancouver sit at the top of the range; smaller cities run 15–25% lower.

## Typical hourly rates by Canadian metro

Across Canadian metros, the **2026 residential range for a licensed plumber** is roughly **CA$80–$160/hour** for labour. Apprentices bill lower; master plumbers and specialists (gas-fitter ticket, backflow certified) bill higher. Rates below are widely reported ranges as of early 2026 — confirm with two local quotes before budgeting.

| Metro | Typical range (CAD/hr) | Median |
|---|---|---|
| Toronto / GTA | $100–$170 | $130 |
| Vancouver / Lower Mainland | $100–$170 | $135 |
| Calgary | $90–$150 | $115 |
| Edmonton | $85–$145 | $110 |
| Ottawa | $90–$140 | $115 |
| Montreal | $80–$140 | $110 |
| Halifax | $80–$130 | $105 |
| Winnipeg | $80–$130 | $105 |
| Smaller cities / rural | $65–$110 | $90 |

Where rates push higher than these ranges without a specialty (gas-line work, commercial backflow, condo high-rise), get a second quote.

## Service call fees and minimums

Canadian shops bill similarly to U.S. shops, with three components:

**Service call (or "trip") fee.** Typically **$75–$150 CAD**. Covers the dispatch and the drive. Some shops roll the first 30 minutes of work into the fee; others start the clock on arrival.

**One-hour minimum.** Most shops won't dispatch for less. A 20-minute job still bills the minimum.

**Parts markup.** 20%–50% above wholesale is standard. A $40 cartridge becomes $55–$60 on the invoice. Bringing your own parts is sometimes negotiable on minor jobs but often refused on warrantied work.

For a typical one-hour residential fixture repair in a major Canadian metro, the all-in math looks like this:

| Line item | Amount (CAD) |
|---|---|
| Trip / service call | $100 |
| Labour (1 hour) | $120 |
| Parts | $60 |
| **Subtotal** | **$280** |
| HST (13%, Ontario) | $36 |
| **All-in** | **$316** |

That's a representative Toronto or Ottawa number. Vancouver and most BC cities use GST 5% + PST 7% (12%); Alberta is GST 5% only; Quebec is GST 5% + QST 9.975% (~15%).

## Provincial sales tax notes

The headline rate matters less than what taxes get added.

- **Ontario, New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, PEI, Newfoundland:** 13–15% HST on labour and parts.
- **British Columbia:** 5% GST + 7% PST = 12%.
- **Alberta, NWT, Nunavut, Yukon:** 5% GST only — lowest combined tax burden.
- **Quebec:** 5% GST + 9.975% QST ≈ 15%.
- **Saskatchewan, Manitoba:** 5% GST + provincial sales tax (6% SK, 7% MB).

A quote that says "$120/hour" without specifying tax treatment can land 15% higher than expected. Always confirm whether the rate is HST-included or HST-extra. Cash-discount offers ("we can do it for $X if you don't need a receipt") are HST-evasion, which voids any warranty and any insurance recourse if the work later fails. Skip.

## What typical jobs cost in Canadian metros

<PullQuote>The best predictor of a fair quote isn't the hourly rate — it's whether the plumber tells you how long the job will take *before* they start.</PullQuote>

For Toronto / GTA at mid-range rates, with HST included:

- **Toilet repair (flapper, fill valve, supply line):** $200–$350.
- **Toilet replacement:** $400–$700 all-in.
- **Faucet repair:** $200–$400.
- **Faucet replacement (homeowner-supplied):** $300–$500.
- **Drain clog (kitchen / bathroom):** $200–$400.
- **Drain clog (main line, requires camera or snake auger):** $400–$1,200.
- **Water heater replacement (40–50 gal gas or electric):** $1,800–$3,000.
- **Shutoff valve replacement:** $300–$500.
- **Burst-pipe emergency repair:** $500–$1,500+ depending on access.

Vancouver, Calgary, and Ottawa typically run 5–10% lower. Montreal and Halifax run 10–20% lower. Rural and smaller-city rates are 20–30% lower but with smaller selection — sometimes only one shop in town can take an emergency call within 48 hours.

## Emergency and after-hours rates

- **Evening (after 5 pm) and Saturday:** 1.5× standard.
- **Sunday and statutory holidays:** 2× standard.
- **Overnight (10 pm–7 am):** 2× to 3× standard.

For a Toronto plumber at $130/hr standard, an overnight visit can clear $300/hour plus a higher trip fee. A typical 90-minute overnight emergency visit is often $700–$1,200 CAD all-in.

Winter is the season that breaks Canadian plumbing budgets. Frozen-pipe burst calls peak from late December through February. Some shops add a winter surcharge or run an emergency-only schedule during cold snaps. Booking a service contract or maintenance plan in autumn is a real hedge in cold-climate cities.

## When you can wait until morning

- **Slow drip you can localize, with a bucket underneath.** Wait.
- **Toilet that won't shut off but you've turned the local stop.** Wait.
- **Water heater drip from a fitting you've contained.** Wait.

When *not* to wait:

- **Active flow that's reaching framing, drywall, or electrical.** Shut the main and call. Our [pipe-burst first 10 minutes](/emergency-repairs/pipe-burst-first-10-minutes) playbook applies regardless of which side of the border you're on.
- **Sewage backup in a finished area.** Cannot wait — biohazard.
- **Water near the electrical panel.** Call your utility's 24-hour line; do not approach the panel.

## How to compare Canadian plumber quotes

Same playbook as our [three-contractor-quotes guide](/advice/three-contractor-quotes), with these Canada-specific additions:

1. **Confirm provincial licensing.** Skilled Trades Ontario, SkilledTradesBC, CMMTQ, etc. A licensed plumber must hold a Certificate of Qualification (Red Seal in plumbing is the federal portable credential). Ask for the licence number. Verify on the provincial registry.
2. **Confirm WSIB / WorkSafeBC coverage** for the workers on your property. If they don't have it and someone is injured, you can be liable.
3. **Confirm insurance** — at least $2M in general liability. Standard for any legitimate shop.
4. **Confirm tax handling** — quote should state "HST extra" or "HST included." Cash-discount offers should be a hard no.
5. **Get the quote in writing.** Verbal quotes drift; written ones don't.

## Smaller-city and rural pricing reality

In smaller centres (Kingston, Saskatoon, Moncton, Kelowna, etc.), the hourly rate drops 15–25% but the choice narrows. Often only 2–4 shops serve a given town. That means:

- Less competitive pricing pressure than major metros.
- Longer waits for non-emergency work (weeks, not days, during busy seasons).
- Travel charges added if you're outside a primary service zone.
- The owner-operator shop is often the best value once you find one with a strong reputation locally.

Online review platforms (HomeStars, Google Reviews) help filter quality in smaller markets where word-of-mouth is the dominant trust signal.

## A note on insurance

Most Canadian homeowner policies cover "sudden and accidental" water damage from internal plumbing — a burst pipe, a failed appliance hose, a one-time overflow. Most do **not** cover gradual seepage (slow leaks that went undetected for weeks). Sewer backup coverage is often a separate endorsement that's worth the small premium add-on. The Insurance Bureau of Canada notes water damage is now the leading homeowner claim in Canada — if you don't have leak detectors and sewer-backup coverage on a finished basement, both are worth pricing this year.
