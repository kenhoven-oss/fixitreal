Faucet replacement is one of the most common residential plumbing jobs. The labor is straightforward; the cost variation comes almost entirely from how nice a faucet you pick and how corroded the shutoff valves are when the plumber opens up the cabinet.

<QuickAnswer>
A like-for-like faucet swap runs **$120–$300 DIY** or **$250–$525 hired**. Plumber labor is typically **$140–$240** (often at a 1- or 2-hour minimum). The faucet itself adds **$50–$400+** depending on what you pick. Any quote over **$650** without a specific reason (rotted countertop substrate, new shutoff valves required, custom configuration) is overpriced.

## DIY vs. hire

A faucet replacement is genuinely DIY-friendly for most homeowners if the shutoff valves under the sink actually shut off. The hard part is access (lying on your back under the sink), not the work. Two patterns to watch for:

- **Shutoff valves are corroded or seized.** This converts the job from "swap a faucet" to "shut off the house water at the main, replace the valves, then swap the faucet" — much more work, much higher chance of needing a plumber. Test the valves *before* you commit to DIY: turn each off, open the faucet, confirm zero flow. If a valve weeps or won't close fully, you're hiring.
- **The mounting nuts are corroded onto the threaded posts.** Basin wrenches and PB Blaster help, but if you're an hour in and the nut hasn't budged, a plumber with a long-handled basin wrench and patience does this all day.

If everything moves smoothly, plan on 90 minutes for a first-time DIY and $40 in tools (basin wrench, plumber's tape, supply lines). If it doesn't, call a plumber and budget the full hired range.

## What a fair quote should include

A defensible plumber quote for a faucet replacement looks like:

1. **The faucet model** (or "homeowner-supplied" if you're bringing it)
2. **Service-call fee or hourly rate**, plus minimum billed time
3. **New shutoff valves and supply lines as standard inclusions** — these should be in the quote, not appearing as a $40 upcharge mid-job
4. **Haul-away of the old faucet included**
5. **A workmanship warranty** — typically 30–90 days on the install
6. **A note on permit requirements** ("No permit required for like-for-like swap" is fine)

## Red flags

- **Quote over $650 without a specific complication mentioned.** A $750 quote that doesn't say "rotted countertop" or "new shutoff valves required" or "wall-mount conversion" is overpriced.
- **Cash-only request** for a $200–$500 plumbing call. See our [Contractor Quote Checker](/tools/contractor-quote-checker) for the full red-flag list.
- **Pressure to upgrade the faucet** to the plumber's preferred brand mid-quote. They can suggest, but the brand choice is yours.
- **No itemized breakdown.** Lump-sum quotes hide markup. Ask for line items.
